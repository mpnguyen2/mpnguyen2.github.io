Minh Nguyen

 

I am an engineer and researcher working on reinforcement learning, stochastic control theory, and scientific machine learning.

E-mail: minhpnguyen@utexas.edu

Google Scholar · LinkedIn · GitHub

I am currently a PhD Software Engineer in AI/ML at Google, where I work on applied ML problems. Previously, I was a Machine Learning Scientist at Microsoft, working on language modeling and related ML systems. Before that, I completed a PhD in mathematics at the University of Texas at Austin, focusing on stochastic control theory and its applications to machine learning.

I am especially interested in machine learning methods for sequential decision-making, learning under uncertainty, and structured learning.

Recent Work

  • Decoupled Continuous-Time Reinforcement Learning via Hamiltonian Flow
    [Preprint 2026] [Paper] · [Website] · [Code]

    A continuous-time reinforcement learning framework based on decoupled value flow and Hamiltonian operators for efficient learning under irregular time steps.

  • GRL-SNAM: Geometric Reinforcement Learning with Differential Hamiltonians for Navigation and Mapping in Unknown Environments
    [ICLR 2026] [Paper] · [Website] · [Code]

    A differential reinforcement learning approach for navigation and mapping in unknown environments, built around adaptive Hamiltonian structures.

  • A Differential and Pointwise Control Approach to Reinforcement Learning
    [NeurIPS 2025] [Paper] · [Website] · [Code]
    A reinforcement learning theory centered on differential and pointwise control structure, with an emphasis on physics-informed settings.
